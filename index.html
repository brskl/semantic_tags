The Forces of the Universe

Overview

There are four basic forces known in our universe:
  • Strong Nuclear
  • Weak Nuclear
  • Electromagnetic
  • Gravitation

Strong Nuclear force

The Strong Nuclear force only works at nuclear distances and is 137 times strong than the Electromagnetic force. It is responsible for keeping the quarks together in a hadron particle, is carried by gluons, and causes quarks to change color. Indirectly it keeps the nucleons of a nucleus together.

Weak Nuclear force

The Weak Nuclear force only works at nuclear distances and is one-millionth as strong as the Electromagnetic force. It is responsible for quarks changing flavor. The Weak Nuclear force is carried by W and Z bosons. Indirectly it is responsible for radioactivity

Electromagnetic force

The Electromagnetic force works at large distances and follows the inverse-square law. It effects particles with an electric charge such as electrons and protons, causing like charges to repel and unlike charges to attract. The Electomagnetic force is carried by photons

Gravitational force

The Gravitional force works at large distances and follows the inverse-square law. It is 6 * 10 -39 times as strong as the Electromagnetic force. It effects all particles with mass causing them to attract each other. Currently it is unknown if there is a force carrier particle, but the graviton has been hypothesised.
